Watering Hole Attacks on NGOs
In 2014, a group of Chinese hackers compromised the website of the US-based Veterans of Foreign Wars (VFW) and used it to deliver malware to site visitors. This tactic, known as a "watering hole" attack, targeted individuals who were likely to visit the VFW website, such as veterans and government employees.
Phishing Campaigns Impersonating Banks
Malicious websites often impersonate banks and financial institutions. In 2020, a phishing campaign targeted users of a major UK bank, convincing them to enter their credentials on a fake login page, leading to unauthorized access to their accounts.
Fake Cryptocurrency Exchange
In 2018, a fake cryptocurrency exchange called "mybtgwallet" stole over $3 million worth of Bitcoin Gold from users. The website appeared legitimate but was designed to steal users' private keys.

Opening malicious websites can lead to various harmful consequences, as these sites are designed to exploit vulnerabilities in your web browser, operating system, or even your online behavior. Here are some potential consequences of opening malicious websites:

  • Malicious websites may attempt to download and install malware on your device without your knowledge.
  • Some malicious websites are designed to mimic legitimate websites, such as banking or social media sites, to trick you into entering your login credentials or personal information.
  • Certain malicious websites can exploit vulnerabilities in your browser or plugins to automatically download malware onto your device without any interaction or consent from you.
